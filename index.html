Latinos in Motion: Mapping Migration to Atlanta

An Interactive Visualization by Cristina Ramirez Vinaja

Dataset from ACS

↓

Atlanta has emerged as a vital destination for Latinx migrants over the past several decades, reshaping both the city proper and the broader metro region’s cultural, economic, and spatial landscapes.

While traditional gateways like Los Angeles or New York have long dominated migration narratives, Atlanta’s rapidly growing and diverse Latinx population offers a compelling case for understanding contemporary migration in the U.S. South.

This project—Latinx in Motion: Mapping Migration to Atlanta—will combine interactive data visualizations with photographic essays to trace how Latinx communities move through space, establish themselves, and create sites of belonging. By pairing quantitative flows and trends with images of everyday life, the goal is to humanize migration data and reveal both structural patterns and lived experiences.

Atlanta Through My Eyes

This photo essay reflects Latinx life in Atlanta through music, food, family, altars, and labor. These everyday scenes reveal how Latinx migrants build belonging in the city. From shared meals to cultural celebrations, these images show how community, memory, and resilience shape what it means to call Atlanta home.
